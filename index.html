1. Abstract
Conversational Speech Synthesis (CSS) seeks to generate target utterances within a user-agent’s dialogue environment, endowing them with human-like emotional prosody. In real-world conversational scenarios, however, human emotional expression is often complex and blended (such as crying tears of joy). Existing emotional CSS models typically assume that each utterance in the dialogue history carries only a single emotion, and on this basis, they predict a single emotional label for the target utterance. This approach overlooks the nuanced control of mixed emotions, limiting the agent’s ability to synthesize speech with the emotional richness characteristic of human communication. To address the above issues, we propose a novel CSS system with the ability to simultaneously understand and express mixed emotions, termed XEmoTalker. Specifically, we first train a Mixed Emotion Expert, which effectively decouples the seven basic emotions and accurately extracts the mixed emotion distribution (XEmoDist) from a speech. Then, we propose a Hypergraph-based Contextual Mixed Emotion Understanding scheme. This scheme constructs carefully designed homogeneous and heterogeneous hyperedges to model the high-order relationships of multi-source attributes in dialogue history, such as speaker, text, speech, and XEmoDist, to comprehensively understand the mixed emotional context of the dialogue history and accurately predict the XEmoDist of the target utterance. Finally, we design a Mixed Emotion–guided Conversational Speech Expression module, which combines the predicted XEmoDist of the target utterance, the dialogue context, and the target speaker condition constraint to synthesize anthropomorphic emotional speech. Experiments on three benchmarking datasets, which include English and Chinese languages, show that XEmoTalker synthesizes speech with greater mixed emotional expressiveness compared to baseline methods. The code and demos are available at: https://github.com/AI-S2-Lab/XEmoTalker.

3. Comparison with SOTA CSS Methods
Comparative Experiment:
1) BaseCSS introduces a text coarse-grained context encoder to model the dialogue history, aiming to improve the quality of synthesized speech. [PAPER]
2) M²-CTTS proposes a multi-scale, multi-modal context encoder to jointly model both coarse-grained and fine-grained features of the text and speech modalities in multimodal dialogue history, aiming to enhance the prosody of synthesized speech. [PAPER]
3) CONCSS incorporates a negative sample enhancement sampling strategy into multimodal dialogue history modeling to improve the interpretability and prosody sensitivity of context representations, thereby enhancing the prosodic expressiveness of synthesized speech. [PAPER]
4) MSRGCN-CSS introduces a context modeling scheme based on a multi-scale relational graph convolutional network to capture the multi-scale influences of multimodal dialogue history on speaking style, aiming to enhance the speaking style of synthesized speech. [PAPER]
5) ECSS introduces a context modeling scheme based on multi-source knowledge heterogeneous graphs to jointly model speech, text, emotion, emotion intensity, and speaker attributes in dialogue history, enhancing the emotional prediction for target utterances and improving the emotional expressiveness of synthesized speech. [PAPER]
6) I³-CSS proposes an utterance-level intra-modal and inter-modal context interaction modeling scheme to learn both intra-modal and inter-modal interactions between the semantics and prosody of multimodal dialogue history and those of the target utterance, aiming to improve the prosody performance of synthesized speech. [PAPER]
7) MFCIG-CSS proposes a multimodal fine-grained context interaction graph-based CSS framework that constructs a semantic interaction graph and a prosody interaction graph to model word-level semantic and prosodic interactions in multimodal dialogue history, thereby enhancing the natural conversational prosody of synthesized speech. [PAPER]
